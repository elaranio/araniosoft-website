Spending Heatmap

See your money.
Literally.

A visual map of all your spending: every category is a block — its size is how much you spent, its color tells you if you're on track or over. A 3-second glance replaces half an hour of spreadsheets.

Zero-Based Budgeting

Every dollar,
with a purpose.

Assign your income to categories with the 50/20/20/10 rule — Needs, Savings, Wants and Debt — before the month begins. Nothing is left "unassigned": you don't just log expenses, you know if you're heading where you want to go.

Credit cards

Installments,
under control.

Log a buy-now-pay-later purchase once and the app creates each monthly charge in the month it belongs to. Statement cutoff and payment dates are calculated automatically, with per-cycle balances: you know exactly what's coming on your next statement — and how many months you've already committed.

Few apps in the world handle installments like this. ZeroBudget was born with them.

Smart capture

Log expenses
in seconds.

Scan a receipt and the app pulls out the amount for you. Type "Walmart" and it suggests the category, the account, even whether it was an installment — it learns from your habits. Or skip the app entirely: "Hey Siri, add a 500 grocery expense."

Family finances New

Share expenses
via AirDrop.

Supplementary family card? Whoever uses it exports their transactions and sends them via AirDrop or Mail. Whoever pays the statement opens them with ZeroBudget and imports them into their own card, with their own categories. No cloud, no shared accounts — the file travels straight from one iPhone to the other.
